Book Review:

Medical Apartheid: The Dark History of Medical Experimentation on Black Americans from Colonial Times to the Present

By Harriet A. Washington

One of the most disturbing books I have ever read. She touches on pellagra as a byproduct of the malnutrition of the enslaved and the fascinating pseudoscience of how it was written off as a "sexual affliction" when it mostly affected poor black sharecroppers. It was only widely understood as a nutritional deficiency when a famine caused it to affect white farmers.

Wernicke-Korsakoff Syndrome

A neurological disorder caused by vitamin B1 (thiamine) deficiency, typically associated with alcoholism, and characterized by confusion, abnormal eye movements, and unsteady gait.

Treatment

First line of treatment is oral or intravenous thiamine. Side affects include alcohol withdrawal symptoms, insomnia, sweating, mood swings, confusion, hallucinations and agitation. A balanced diet and sobriety is also recommended.

Demographics

Alcoholics.

Diagnosis

Liver function test to detect alcoholism, serum albumin test (low levels signal nutritional deficiencies, kidney problems or liver problems), serum vitamin B-1 test (low enzyme activity in RBCs signal thiamine deficiency), EKG before and after thiamine supplement to check for improvement, CT for brain lesions and neuropsychological testing to determine severity of mental deficiencies.

Pellagra

Nicotinic acid or tryptophan deficiency causes dermatitis, diarrhea and mental disturbance.

Symptoms

Delusions, mental confusion, diarrhea, nausea, inflamed mucous membranes, scaly skin sores.

Cause

Niacin deficiency.

Diagnosis

Nutritional testing of blood or urine.

Treatment

Niacin, balanced diet.

Demographics

People with homogenous diets (poor farmers), alcoholics, people with digestive disorders, people taking tuberculosis drug isoniazid (Laniazid, Nydrazid).

Beri-Beri

Peripheral nervous system and cardiovascular symptoms caused by a vitamin B1 (thiamine) deficiency.

Symptoms

Muscle weakness, headache, coordination problems, muscle wasting, tachycardia, cardiomegaly, possible: peripheral neuropathy, shortness of breath, swelling in extremities, pins and needles.

Prognosis and Duration

Life expectancy is not affected.

Cause

Lack of foods like whole grains, meat, fish, poultry, eggs, milk products, green leafy vegetables, legumes, orange or tomato juices. Associated with a cassava-dependent diet.

Treatment

Thiamine supplements, nutritionally balanced diet.

Demographics

More prevalent in the developing world.

Diagnosis

Blood and urine tests to measure thiamine levels (low concentration in blood, high concentration in urine), neurological exam, physical exam for heart problems.

Sensorimotor Polyneuropathy

A general degeneration of peripheral nerves that spreads towards the center of the body.

Symptoms

Decreased feeling, difficulty swallowing, breathing, using arms, hands, legs or feet, walking, neuralgia, weakness of the face, arms or legs.

Prognosis and Duration

Life expectancy is not affected.

Cause

Alcoholic neuropathy, paraneoplastic syndrome, diabetes, drug complications, Gulliane-Barre syndrome, genetic disorders, vitamind B12 deficiency or vitamin E deficiency.

Treatment

Dependent on the cause.

Demographics

Those with alcoholic neuropathy, paraneoplastic syndrome, diabetes, drug complications, Gulliane-Barre syndrome, genetic disorders, vitamind B12 deficiency or vitamin E deficiency.

Diagnosis

Blood testing, EMG, or nerve conduction studies.
